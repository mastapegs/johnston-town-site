# Johnston Community Directory — Vision

## The Problem

Johnston, Rhode Island is a town of roughly 30,000 people, but there's no single, reliable place for residents to find the services they need. Looking for a food bank? You'll end up scrolling through outdated Google results and 211 listings that may or may not still be accurate. Need affordable childcare? Good luck piecing together information from Facebook groups and word of mouth.

This isn't a technology problem. It's a community problem. The resources exist — they're just scattered, hard to find, and impossible to keep track of.

## What This Is

The Johnston Community Directory is a free, publicly accessible website that connects Johnston residents (and anyone who needs it) with local services, resources, and businesses — all in one place.

It starts simple: a curated, well-organized directory of essential services in Johnston, RI (02919). Over time, it grows into something the whole community can rely on and contribute to.

## Who It's For

- **Residents** looking for services — a parent searching for daycare, a family that needs a food bank, someone trying to figure out which office at Town Hall handles their problem
- **Newcomers** moving to Johnston who need to get oriented
- **Business owners** who want to be found by their neighbors
- **Community organizations** that want to make sure the people who need them can actually find them

## What Matters Most — Essential Services First

The first priority is the things people truly need:

- Food banks and pantries
- Childcare and daycare
- Healthcare and dental clinics
- Senior services
- Shelters and housing assistance
- Mental health and substance abuse resources
- Municipal services (Town Hall, public works, schools, libraries, parks)

Local businesses — restaurants, shops, auto repair, salons — will follow. But the foundation is built on helping people find help.

## How It Works

### Phase 1 — Curated Launch
Listings are researched and added by hand. Every entry is verified. Quality and accuracy matter more than quantity. The goal is to be trustworthy from day one.

### Phase 2 — Community Input
Open up a way for community members to suggest new listings or flag outdated information. Submissions are reviewed before going live to maintain quality.

### Phase 3 — Sustainable Growth
Offer local businesses the option to claim and enhance their listings — better placement, richer profiles, photos. This creates a revenue path that keeps the directory free for residents while giving businesses a reason to participate.

## What Each Listing Should Include

This will evolve, but at minimum:

- Name
- Address
- Phone number
- Website (if applicable)
- Hours of operation
- Category

Over time, the kind of details that actually help people — things like whether a food bank requires ID, whether a daycare accepts subsidies, whether a place has wheelchair access or bilingual staff. The details Google doesn't tell you.

## Guiding Principles

1. **Community first.** Every decision should be measured by whether it helps Johnston residents find what they need.
2. **Accuracy over volume.** A small directory that's correct is better than a large one that's wrong.
3. **Free to use, always.** The directory itself will never charge residents to access information.
4. **Built by a neighbor.** This project comes from someone who lives here and sees the gaps firsthand. That perspective matters and should stay at the center of every choice.

## The Dream

Johnston is a good town. The people here look out for each other. But right now, too much of that help depends on knowing the right person or being in the right Facebook group. This directory is a small step toward making sure that when someone in Johnston needs something, they can find it — easily, reliably, and without jumping through hoops.

This is just the beginning.
